William Shakespeare was born in Stratford-upon-Avon, England, in April 1564. He was the third of eight children in his family. His father, John Shakespeare, was a prosperous merchant, and his mother, Mary Arden, came from a prominent family. William received a basic education and was introduced to literature at an early age.

Shakespeare's career in the theater began when he joined the Lord Chamberlain's Men, a prominent acting company in London. He wrote numerous plays that became immensely popular, including "Romeo and Juliet," "Hamlet," and "Macbeth." His works are still performed worldwide and are considered some of the greatest literary achievements in the English language.

Despite his success as a playwright, Shakespeare faced personal challenges, including the loss of his son, Hamnet. He married Anne Hathaway, and they had three children together. Shakespeare is known for his sonnets, many of which explore themes of love, beauty, and time. He retired to Stratford-upon-Avon and passed away in 1616.

William Shakespeare's legacy is profound. His works have left an indelible mark on literature, theater, and the English language. He is often regarded as the greatest playwright in history. The annual celebrations of his life and works, including Shakespearean plays, continue to captivate audiences around the world.
